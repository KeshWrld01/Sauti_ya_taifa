Your Rights

Every Kenyan has rights. Know them. Use them. Protect them.

⚖️ Rights During Arrest

When police arrest you, the law is on your side — but only if you know it. Here is exactly what the Constitution of Kenya 2010 guarantees you:

Right to be Informed

You must be told the reason for your arrest immediately, in a language you understand. (Article 49(1)(a))

Right to Remain Silent

You have the right to remain silent. Anything you say can be used against you. You do not have to answer questions without a lawyer. (Article 49(1)(d))

Right to a Lawyer

You have the right to communicate with an advocate of your choice. If you cannot afford one, the state must provide one in serious cases. (Article 49(1)(c))

Right Not to Be Tortured

No one may subject you to any form of violence, torture, or cruel treatment. This is absolute — no exceptions. (Article 29(f))

Right to Be Brought to Court

You must be brought before a court within 24 hours of arrest. If arrested on a holiday or weekend, the next working day. (Article 49(1)(f))

Right to Bail

You have the right to apply for bail or bond. A court can only deny bail in exceptional circumstances. (Article 49(1)(h))

✊ Rights During Protest

Peaceful protest is a constitutional right in Kenya. Article 37 of the Constitution states clearly:

"Every person has the right, peaceably and unarmed, to assemble, to demonstrate, to picket, and to present petitions to public authorities." — Constitution of Kenya 2010, Article 37

You Can Protest Without a Permit

The law does not require protesters to obtain a permit. Police cannot arrest you simply for protesting peacefully.

Police Cannot Use Excessive Force

Police are only allowed to use force proportional to the threat. Shooting live bullets at unarmed protesters is illegal and unconstitutional.

Right to Film & Document

You have the right to film police and public officials in public spaces. They cannot legally take your phone or demand you delete footage.

Right to Medical Attention

If you are injured during a protest, you have the right to immediate medical attention. Police cannot block access to emergency care.

🚫 What Police CANNOT Do

  • ❌ Arrest you without telling you why
  • ❌ Hold you for more than 24 hours without taking you to court
  • ❌ Deny you access to a lawyer
  • ❌ Torture or assault you in custody
  • ❌ Search your home without a warrant (except in limited circumstances)
  • ❌ Take your phone or belongings without a proper legal process
  • ❌ Shoot at unarmed, peaceful protesters
  • ❌ Threaten or intimidate you into confessing
