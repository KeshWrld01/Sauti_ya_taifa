📜 What is the Constitution?

The Constitution of Kenya 2010 is the supreme law of the land. No person, institution, or government can act against it. It was born from the struggle of the Kenyan people and belongs to every one of us. Article 2(1) states clearly: "This Constitution is the supreme law of the Republic and binds all persons and all State organs at both levels of government."

🛡️ The Bill of Rights

Chapter 4 of the Constitution contains the Bill of Rights — a declaration of what every Kenyan is entitled to, simply by being human.

Right to Life (Article 26)

Every person has the right to life. The government must protect your life and cannot take it arbitrarily.

Right to Equality (Article 27)

Every person is equal before the law. Discrimination based on race, sex, ethnicity, religion, or social status is prohibited.

Freedom of Expression (Article 33)

Every person has the right to freedom of expression including the right to seek, receive, and impart information.

Right to Assembly (Article 37)

Every person has the right to peaceably assemble, demonstrate, picket, and present petitions to public authorities.

Right to Education (Article 53)

Every child has the right to free and compulsory basic education. This is a duty of the state.

Right to Health (Article 43)

Every person has the right to the highest attainable standard of health, including reproductive health care.

🏛️ How Government is Structured

Kenya operates on a system of separation of powers across three arms of government:

The Executive

Led by the President, Deputy President, and Cabinet. Responsible for implementing laws and running government affairs.

The Legislature (Parliament)

Made up of the National Assembly and Senate. Makes laws, approves budgets, and oversees the executive.

The Judiciary

Made up of courts including the Supreme Court. Interprets the law and protects constitutional rights.

🌍 Devolution

Kenya has 47 counties, each with its own government. Devolution was designed to bring power, resources, and services closer to the people. County governments are led by Governors and County Assemblies. They control a portion of national revenue and are responsible for local services like healthcare, early education, and agriculture.

"The object of devolution is to give powers of self-governance to the people and enhance their participation in the exercise of the powers of the State." — Constitution of Kenya 2010, Article 174

📢 How to Hold Leaders Accountable

  • ✅ Vote in every election — national and county
  • ✅ Attend public participation forums — they are mandatory by law
  • ✅ File petitions to Parliament or your County Assembly
  • ✅ Use the Freedom of Information Act to request government data
  • ✅ Report corruption to the Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC)
  • ✅ Use courts to challenge unconstitutional actions
  • ✅ Engage civil society and human rights organizations
